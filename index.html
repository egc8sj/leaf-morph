Leaf Morphology by Latitude

This visualization presents selected results from the 2012 study, "Leaf morphology shift linked to climate change" by Greg R. Guerin, Haixia Wen, and Andrew J. Lowe. The study compared leaf widths, lengths, and surface areas on Dodonaea viscosa subsp. angustissima in the Adelaide Geosyncline region, South Australia. Among other results, the study found that leaf width corollated negatively with latitude; leaves farther north within the region were narrower than leaves farther south.

REFERENCE
Guerin G. R., Wen H., Lowe A. J. 2012. Leaf morphology shift linked to climate change. Biology Letters 8(5). 10.1098/rsbl.2012.0458 https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pubmed/22764114
